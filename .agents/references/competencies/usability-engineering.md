### Usability Engineering

- **Heuristic Evaluation:** Systematic inspection of interfaces against established heuristics (Nielsen's 10, Shneiderman's 8 Golden Rules) to identify usability problems without user testing
- **Usability Testing:** Planning and facilitating moderated and unmoderated usability tests, defining task scenarios, measuring task success, time-on-task, error rates, and satisfaction
- **Cognitive Walkthroughs:** Step-by-step evaluation of learnability by simulating a new user's problem-solving process at each interface step
- **Accessibility Assessment:** Evaluating conformance with WCAG guidelines (Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust) and testing with assistive technologies
- **Interaction Analysis:** Analyzing click paths, scroll depth, form abandonment, and user flows to identify friction points and drop-off patterns
- **Benchmark Measurement:** Establishing and tracking UX metrics — System Usability Scale (SUS), task completion rates, error rates, time-on-task — over time to quantify improvement
- **Information Architecture:** Card sorting (open and closed), tree testing, and first-click analysis to evaluate findability and navigation structures
